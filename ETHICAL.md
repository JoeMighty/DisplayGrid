# Ethical Use Policy

DisplayGrid is open source software released under the MIT Licence.
It was built for restaurants, churches, schools, event venues, and
community organisations — places where people gather.

## Intended Use

DisplayGrid is designed for:

- Restaurants, cafes, and hospitality venues
- Churches, mosques, temples, and places of worship
- Schools, universities, and educational institutions
- Conference centres, auditoriums, and community spaces
- Retail and small business signage
- Non-profit and charitable organisations

## Uses We Do Not Welcome

While the MIT Licence does not legally restrict any use, the author
explicitly does not want DisplayGrid used for:

- Military operations, installations, or equipment
- Weapons development, targeting systems, or surveillance infrastructure
- Law enforcement surveillance, crowd monitoring, or facial recognition
- Border control or immigration enforcement systems
- Propaganda or disinformation campaigns
- Any application designed to harm, control, or monitor people
  without their knowledge or consent

These are not legally enforceable restrictions. They are a clear
statement of intent. If your use case falls into the categories above,
you are asked — sincerely — not to use this software.

## In Short

Build things that bring people together. Use this software in spaces
where communities gather, celebrate, learn, and eat.

Not in spaces designed to monitor, harm, or control them.
